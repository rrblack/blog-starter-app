<h1 className="text-white">Welcome back after nearly <span className="text-red-600"> 2 years </span> </h1>

This has definitely been the longest I have gone without a blog post. But it is for a good reason. I have been super busy ever since my last update almost 2 years ago. To start we are now in IT. Yes that's right, I am no longer a teacher, no longer an editor. I am in IT. Working at the lowest tier of IT currently, mind you, but we are out here. I have a week of night shift in a row every month. And it just so happens that week is my night shift week. So while I am currently working this great shift this week, I figured I would get my first blog post out on my new site up.

Speaking of this site, what do you guys think? We are no longer on WordPress. It's even better; I built this site completely by myself. That's right, this site was built entirely by me from the ground up. Sure I used a starting template, but everything you see here is specifically because I wanted it to be there. I designed every part that you see on this site. From the comment section to the mailing list, title sizes, you name it. Working in IT has been a propelling force that has pushed me to constantly want to learn and create new things. With my current role in IT being fairly easy, there is a lot of downtime. And in that downtime I am busy working away at studying for certifications, learning how to program a site or set up my own completely self hosted photo library. I have a ton of projects in the works and everyday is a new adventure. This blog was just one of many I plan on doing. 

Also, since this site is completely owned AND hosted by me now. That means no more yearly $100 fees from WordPress, no more slow site loading, no more targeted advertising. If there are any features you guys want on the site leave me a comment and I will literally create it and add it on the site. There are no longer any limits. For any nerds who are curious what language I am running this site on, it is written in Next.js, TypeScript and MDX. Also for those who were wondering where my old blogs all went: <b className="text-red-500"> they are still all here, don't worry! </b> You can access them on the <a href="/">front page </a> on this site. While I currently don't have plans to migrate them to this site, it is not out of the realm of possibility in the eventual future. Keep in mind this blog is still a work in progress and I will be continuously maintaining it and adding new features when I can.

And yes, for anyone still wondering, I am still in the same relationship I was back in my first blog post. Yukina and I have been together for nearly 2 years now. December 17th will be our 2 year anniversary. I don't think I would have made it this far without her help and support. She was the one after all who suggested that I enter IT in the first place. She also helped and supported me tremendously when I was unemployed trying to get a job in IT. 

Normally this is the part where I move on to the next topic and sweep my feelings under the rug. But we're not going to do that anymore. Let me say exactly how I feel about Yukina just so everyone is clear.

<b className="text-red-500"> Yukina is one of the greatest things that has ever happened in my life. </b> I don't think there is anyone in this world that understands me better than she does. And I truly mean that. We have spent a tremendous amount of time together these past 2 years and we both know each other inside and out. Of course there are the occasional fights and arguments but we decided at the end of every argument to both say "I'm sorry" at the exact same time and move on from it. Before meeting her I didn't have much direction in life. I was constantly negative and had depressive thoughts often. After meeting her my life has completely changed for the better. She is not afraid to call me out when I start talking negative and is always a light in my life helping me to look on the positive side for each situation.  

She's also incredibly smart, funny and of course beautiful, both inside and out. She's kind to both animals and everyone she meets and never makes you feel less than. She is a super great cook and can literally recreate any kind of food, just by eating it once. She's also...

You probably guessed it but, this is an art exhibit. It opens up every year in September which is known as the "月見" month in Japan, AKA the month for viewing the full moon. I had wanted to go to this exhibit 2 years ago but never found the time. This year I had the day off that day and figured there'd be no better time now than to also go with Yukina.

Up until recently it has been super hot in Japan. So hot that it wasn't even fun to go out and do anything. My days from July to middle September literally consisted of going to work and coming home. Weekends were also just indoors. And that's not just me, that was most people living in Japan. Compared to Tennessee weather back in America, Japan summers are a completely different beast with literally 90% humidity days. Anyway, because of the summer I hadn't really gone out, but now that it's finally Fall, I expect to be doing some more interesting things in the near future. And of course, taking more photos and posting it here on my blog.
